Frequently Asked Questions

What are your long-term goals, and how do you measure success?

Our long-term goal is to transform the lives of street children by providing permanent access to shelter, education, healthcare, and life skills. We measure success by tracking reintegration into society, school attendance, and personal development progress.

What impact have you made so far?

We have initiated outreach programs to build trust with street children and understand their needs, laying the groundwork for long-term support and rehabilitation efforts.

How will you sustain your work in the long term?

Through income-generating initiatives like vocational training and small business development, we aim to create self-sustaining programs that reduce dependency on external funding.

How do you manage and report the use of funds?

We implement strict financial tracking systems and share regular, transparent reports with our donors to ensure accountability and trust.

How do you prevent misuse of resources?

Our internal controls include a dedicated finance team, clear documentation of all transactions, and periodic audits to ensure responsible use of resources.

Who leads the organization?

We are led by a team of experienced professionals and volunteers passionate about social change, supported by advisors in education, IT, and community development.

Do you have a board or advisors?

Yes, we are establishing a board of advisors composed of experts who will provide strategic direction and ensure transparent decision-making.

Is your organization officially registered?

We are currently in the legal registration process and working with the appropriate authorities to ensure full compliance.

Are you authorized to operate in a refugee setting?

Yes, we are coordinating with local leadership and relevant agencies to ensure all our activities align with regulations and community needs.

How do you track each child’s progress?

Each child will have a personalized development plan, and we will maintain regular records in education, health, and social behavior to monitor progress.

How do you evaluate your programs?

Our evaluation process includes feedback from children and caregivers, performance metrics, and routine reviews to continuously improve our services.

Have you worked with any organizations before?

We are currently forming partnerships with local churches, NGOs, and leaders who share our mission and values.

What values do you seek in a partner?

We value collaboration with partners who are compassionate, transparent, and committed to empowering vulnerable children and communities.

How is the local community involved in your work?

The community plays a vital role by identifying at-risk children, volunteering, and helping us shape culturally appropriate programs.

Are children and families included in decision-making?

Absolutely. We believe in a child-centered approach where families and children are key stakeholders in shaping their own futures.
